Dressings

Types

  1. Vinaigrettes
  2. Mayonnaise based
  3. Dairy based

Serving

  • Shake dressing well before using
  • Only dress when ready to eat so salad does not wilt

Vinaigrettes

  • Ratio: 3 oil to 1 vinegar
  • Combine by shaking in bottle
  • Olive oil goes bitter if whisked, better to shake ingredients together
  • Oil makes leaves wilt and vinegar runs off leaves if no surfactant / emulsifier
  • For mustard: Dijon > English > wholegrain
  • Emulsifiers: mustard, honey, egg yolk
  • Vinegar dressing keeps for 6 months, citrus dressings for 6 weeks
  • Can replace some vinegar with soy sauce or water
  • Rice vinegar can substitute for sherry vinegar
  • Citrus or sherry vinegar for milder vinaigrette

Honey Dijon

  • Makes 85ml
  • Mix
    • 3 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 tbsp sherry vinegar
    • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
    • 1 tsp honey

Red wine

  • Makes 85ml
  • Mix
    • 3 tbsp olive oil
    • 2 tsp red wine vinegar
    • 1 tsp water
    • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
    • 1 small shallot finely minced
    • ½ clove garlic grated
    • ¼ tsp black pepper
    • ¼ tsp salt

Balsamic

  • Mix
    • 3 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
    • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
    • ½ clove garlic grated
    • ½ tsp maple syrup / honey
    • ¼ tsp salt

Balsamic & soy

  • Makes 85ml
  • Mix
    • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar
    • 40ml (36g) rape seed oil
    • 20ml (18g) olive oil
    • 1¼ tsp Dijon mustard
    • 1 tsp soy
    • ½ small shallot finely minced
    • ½ small clove garlic grated
    • ⅙ tsp salt
    • black pepper

Lime & honey

  • Mix
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
    • 1 tbsp lime juice
    • 1 tbsp honey
    • 1 tsp sherry vinegar
    • ½ tsp Dijon mustard
    • ¼ tsp light brown sugar
    • ¼ tsp cumin
    • black pepper
    • salt

Dill & mustard

  • Too much oil and too runny
  • Dry dill gives no flavour
  • Try sherry vinegar
  • Mix
    • 1 tsp olive oil
    • 1 tsp honey
    • 1 tsp cider vinegar
    • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
    • fresh dill chopped
    • ¼ tsp salt
