Pastry

Notes

  • Pastry easier to roll if chilled as flat disk
  • Do not need to butter tart case edges as pastry while shrink away during cooking
  • Leave overhang around tin top for shrinkage while cooking
  • Cna trim edges by rolling the rolling pin over the tart tin
  • Can accept pastry shrinkage if don't need pastry up to edge of tin
  • Removing paper and beans halfway through cooking dries out the base
  • Pastry case better under baked than over because will cook again
  • Trim edges with sharp knife after cooking while pastry still hot
  • Place tart tin on baking tray to cook so do not damage pastry when moving it
  • Save some uncooked pastry if need to fill in broken edges

Pastry

  1. Blitz fine
    • 175g plain flour
    • 95g unsalted butter chilled & cubed
    • 40g icing sugar
    • pinch salt
  2. Add and pulse to ball
    • 1 egg yolk
    • 1 tbsp cold water
  3. Add more water if dry
  4. Shape as flattened disc with smooth edges on floured surface
  5. Cover and chill for 3 hours

Base

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C
  2. Roll pastry to 2-3mm thin on well-floured surface
  3. Line base of 23cm fluted tart tin with circle of baking paper
  4. Line tin with pastry
  5. Trim edge with sharp knife leaving overhang
  6. Prick all over with fork
  7. Line with scrunched up baking paper
  8. Fill with baking beans
  9. Place tart on baking tray
  10. Bake for 20 mins at 170°C
  11. Remove paper and beans
  12. Bake for 15 mins at 170°C
  13. Brush with little egg white
  14. Bake for 1 min at 170°C
  15. Trim edges while still hot
