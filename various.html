Reference

  • Temperature °F to °C

Notes

  • Heat pan before adding oil then heat oil then add food
  • Good ingredient + good equipment + good technique = good food
  • You can't make a good sandwich out of bad bread
  • Fridge coldest back of bottom and top shelves. Warmest middle of the door
  • Cut meat perpendicular to grain to maximise tenderness
  • Add acids at end because cooking dulls their flavour
  • Toss food in oil because it gives better heat distribution than air and reduces water loss
  • When pan frying, add butter too soon and it can burn going acrid and bitter
  • Heat butter and oil until butter melted and foaming subsides
  • Reduce wine before adding other liquids
  • Maillard reactions carbohydrate and protein 175°C and above
  • Caramelisation only with carbohydrate
  • Beef: 54°C medium-rare, 60°C medium

Pans

  • Aluminium pan discolour and pot with acidic ingredients
  • Tri layer pans best conduction and heat capacity
  • Heavier pans retain more heat

Carbs

  • potatoes, rice, buckwheat, polenta, pearl barley, oats, quinoa
  • Wheat: pasta, bread, couscous, bulgar wheat
