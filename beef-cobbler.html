Beef Cobbler

  • Preheat oven to 140°C

Beef

  1. Season beef 1 hour before cooking
    • 1 tsp salt
    • black pepper
  2. Brown 800g stewing steak in 2 batches
    1. Heat 1 tbsp vegetable oil in pan
    2. Add 1 clove garlic grated
    3. Fry beef for 5 mins until brown
    4. Transfer to casserole dish
  3. Deglaze the pan with and reduce
    • 187ml red wine
  4. Pour wine into casserole dish

Stew

  1. Add to 24cm (4.2l) casserole dish
    • 2 carrots
    • 10 shallots
    • 350ml stock
    • 200ml tomato passata
    • 3 bay leaves
    • 1 star anise in tea ball
  2. Bring to simmer on hob
  3. Cook in oven at 140°C for 2½ hours
  4. Mix and add to thicken if necessary
    • 1 tsp cornflour
    • 2 tsp cold water

Scones

  1. Add to bowl
    • 180g self-raising flour
    • ½ tsp salt
    • ½ tsp baking powder (new)
  2. Rub in until resembles course breadcrumbs
    • 75g butter chilled and cubed
  3. Stir in
    • 75g Cheddar grated
    • 1 tsp English mustard
  4. Beat separately
    • 125ml milk
    • 1 large egg
  5. Stir milk into flour mix until soft dough
  6. Roll into equal 9 scone-sized circles
  7. Brush tops with beaten egg

Cobbler

  1. Turn oven up to 200°C
  2. Remove star anise and bay leaves
  3. Place scones on top of stew
  4. Bake uncovered in oven for 20-25 mins until golden brown

Serving

  • Mange tout, peas
  • Brown rice / mash

Notes

  1. Made: 30 Dec 2022, used 1 Knorr chicken stock pot, beef no flavour because not salted, maybe add herbs as well as star anise
