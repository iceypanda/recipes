Beef Cobbler

  • Preheat oven to 140°C

Beef

  1. Season beef 1 hour before cooking
    • ½ tsp salt
    • black pepper
  2. Brown 1000g stewing steak in 2 batches so not to crowd pan
    1. Heat 1 tbsp vegetable oil in pan
    2. 2 cloves garlic grated (each batch)
    3. Fry beef for 5 mins until brown
    4. Transfer to casserole dish
  3. Deglaze the pan with and reduce
    • 187ml red wine
  4. Pour wine into casserole dish

Stew

  1. Add to 24cm (4.2l) casserole dish
    • 2 carrots half moons
    • 1 parsnip diced (optional)
    • 1 celery stick diced (optional)
    • 10 shallots diced
    • beef oxo cube in 300ml water
    • 300ml tomato passata
    • 4 bay leaves
    • 1 star anise
    • 1 tsp balsamic vinegar
    • 1 tsp light brown sugar
    • ½ tsp thyme
    • ¼ tsp rosemary
    • ¼ tsp fish sauce
    • ¼ tsp soy sauce
  2. Bring to simmer on hob
  3. Cook in oven at 140°C for 2½ hours

Scones

  1. Add to bowl
    • 200g self-raising flour
    • ½ tsp salt
    • ½ tsp baking powder
  2. Blitz to breadcrumbs
    • 75g unsalted butter chilled and cubed
  3. Stir in
    • 75g cheddar grated
    • 1 tsp English mustard
  4. Beat separately
    • 120ml milk
    • 1 large egg
  5. Stir milk into flour mix until soft dough
  6. Roll into equal 9 slightly flattened circles

Cobbler

  1. Turn oven up to 200°C
  2. Remove star anise and bay leaves
  3. Scrape off surface fat
  4. Mix together then add
    • 1 tsp cornflour
    • 2 tsp cold water
  5. Place scones on top of stew, 2 in middle with 7 surrounding in circle
  6. Brush tops with egg yolk
  7. Bake uncovered in oven for 20-25 mins until golden brown

Serving

  • Mash / new potatoes
  • Mange tout, peas
  • Portion 1-2 scones

Notes

  1. Passata increased to 300ml, water reduced to 300ml
