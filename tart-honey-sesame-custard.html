Honey & sesame custard tart

Pastry

  1. Blitz fine
    • 175g plain flour
    • 95g unsalted butter
    • 40g icing sugar
    • pinch salt
  2. Add and pulse to ball
    • 1 egg yolk
    • 1 tbsp cold water
  3. Add more water if dry
  4. Shape as flattened disc on floured surface
  5. Chill 3 hours

Base

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C
  2. Roll pastry to 2-3mm thin on floured surface
  3. Line base of 23cm fluted tart tin with circle of baking paper
  4. Line tin with pastry
  5. Trim edge with sharp knife
  6. Prick all over with fork
  7. Line with scrunched up baking paper
  8. Fill with baking beans
  9. Bake for 20 mins at 170°C
  10. Remove paper and beans
  11. Bake for 15 mins at 170°C
  12. Brush with little egg white
  13. Bake for 1 min at 170°C

Custard

  1. Mix in bowl
    • 4 large egg yolks
    • 1 large egg
  2. Heat in saucepan and heat until steaming
    • 400ml whipping cream
    • 100g fancy honey
  3. Remove heat and add
    • 0.5g salt
  4. Slowly whisk cream mixture into eggs
  5. Leave for 2 mins
  6. Skim off foam
  7. Sieve into jug

Tart

  1. Pour half custard into the pastry base
  2. Transfer to oven
  3. Pour in rest of custard
  4. Bake at 110°C for 40-45 mins
  5. Remove from oven and leave to cool
  6. Cover with plate not with cling film

Topping

  1. Toast
    • 80g sesame seeds
  2. Leave to cool
  3. Sprinkle all over tart
  4. Place around edges of tart with long axis pointing at tart centre
    • 8 quenelle creme fraiche

Serving

  • 12 portions

Notes

  • Original recipe
  • Pastry will shrink when cooked so leave excess around top of tin
  • Pastry easier to roll if chilled as flat disk
  • Removing paper and beans halfway through cooking dries out the base
  • Do not cover with cling film as this will stick to the custard
  • Custard amounts from 6 egg yolks, 600ml cream, 150g honey, 0.7g salt: change depending on amount needed
